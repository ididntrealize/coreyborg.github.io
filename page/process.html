Websites in a Nutshell

Creating purposeful content

How to prepare text for your pages:

Start writing as much as you can without deleting anything. Get as much of your story together into a single document as you can. The easiest way to know what pages you'll need is to get everything together, then divide it up. To brainstorm ideas of what to write, start by listing as many related keywords to your idea as you can. You'll need this list later for SEO, so keep it somewhere you can find it.

Goal oriented conversion

It's important to keep in mind a specific goal for what you want people to do once they come to the site. Each page will be designed to give users the information they need in order to make a choice involving your site. Your site should present viewers with the choice you want them to make once they have been well informed. Some common choices for users to make are:

  • Purchasing a product
  • Subscribing to a newsletter
  • Following on social media
  • Contacting you
What to include in 'Your story'

Make sure to explain why your viewers will benefit from making the choice you want them to make. Describe to them why they will benefit from interacting with your site. For example, you can use this guide to start work on your site by yourself. If you get stuck on any of the steps, you can hire me to push through the most difficult steps!

How to prepare images for your site

Each page on your site will need a mix of pictures and text in order to be enticing. Find photos that help prove the information you are providing your viewers. Start saving them into the same folder where you have prepared text for your pages. Once you've collected many images, it will be easier to pair them with text.

How to prepare a site layout

Once you have an idea for the amount of content to display and the possible interactions users might make, you should layout your images and text together (either on paper, or in a word document). Keep in mind that all your pages should be designed to obviously offer the choice that you want users to make. If your users don’t make that choice immediately, then you should offer relevant information about why that choice is beneficial, and then give them another opportunity to make that choice. Some examples of a choice you might want users to make:

  • Join your newsletter
  • Contact you
  • Buy a product

Deciding to use a website builder

At this point, you need to make a choice of whether to build the site out yourself, or to use a website builder. Using a website builder (like squarespace, wordpress, wix, etc.) is usually what independent businesses choose to do, but it definitely has its trade offs.

  • Squarespace
  • Wordpress
  • Google Sites
  • Wix
The benefits of using a website builder

Building a website using a builder is by far the easiest and most convenient option for building a personal or small business website. The process basically consists of choosing a theme (or template), and filling in your pages using the website builder tools. In wordpress, for example, they have an editor that looks like microsoft word - if you can make it in word, you can make it in wordpress. Then, when you want to make updates to your site, it’s fast and convenient.

The tradeoffs of using website builders

Using website builders isn’t all peaches and cream. For one, most website builders use a freemium model and know just when they can hook you to pay more. Once you’ve committed to one it’s not easy to switch, and you’ll pay more every month for using their plans. Premium themes cost extra as well, and can have certain features turned off unless you pay. Sites built with website builders tend to be formulaic, and it is likely that you won’t be able to get exactly what you want.

Getting the most from your website builder

There are ways to use a website builder, without needing to pay the company extra! However, setting it up yourself can be difficult. I would highly recommend installing wordpress on your own hosting (follow the guide at wordpress.org - NOT wordpress.com). Then once wordpress is set up on your domain, you can access all of the exact same stuff as a site on wordpress.com

Creating custom wordpress themes

Sometimes you just need a pixel-perfect unique design for your site as well as a user-friendly way to update it and host blog authors. And who hasn’t, from time to time? This method is the best of both worlds, you can create exactly what you’re envisioning as HTML templates, and then have wordpress fill in any dynamic content (blog posts, the text on web pages, site title, site headline, etc.). I recommend doing this if you are planning any special functionality for your site, beyond just showing people something pretty.

Creating your site from scratch

Personally, I would be very proud of anybody who took on this task. There’s so much to learn about document formats that you use to tell computers how to visualize your designs. For example, you write an entirely linear file, but the computer has to know it is in two columns! To write that linearly, you would need to learn about HTML and CSS.

Very few people do end up learning about it, though, and that’s why I get paid the big bucks. Additionally, there are many tools that can help you create beautiful and interactive designs without having to dive too deep. After gaining a basic understanding of HTML and CSS, I recommend looking into Twitter Bootstrap, and jQuery.

Domains & Hosting

How servers allow others to see your webpage

When you type yourdomain.com into a web browser (like Chrome or Safari), a lot of things happen automagically behind the scenes. For example, your browser looks up who owns the company which registered the domain (A hosting company). That company owns a computer running 24/7 (a server), designated to send your website to anybody who asks.

What you pay hosting companies for

Your hosting company owns the computer which your server is on, you pay hosting companies for maintenance, easy access to make updates to your site, and additional services. Some common tools they offer are:

  • Custom website builders
  • A secure way to edit your website files
  • Registering domains
  • email @yourdomain.com
  • Database and storage
How to spot freemium traps

It is possible to get hosting and a website builder for free, although they charge far more for necessary premium features that you only notice once you’ve committed to staying with them. Most of the website builders which have a free option are aggressively geared towards you purchasing their full version. I don't recommend using freemium sites for hosting, because once you've started with their tools, you are less likely to switch (and more likely to pay more). Given that addendum, here are some free hosting options:

  • wix.com
  • wordpress.com
  • weebly.com
Free hosting done right

It is possible to host your website for free as well. Some options require that the website is not built with a website builder, like Github. In order to build a website without a website builder, you will have to look into HTML. Other options allow you to use a builder AND still add your own code, like Google Sites. If you are interested in a dynamic website, it can be essential to be able to add your own code to it.

  • Github.com
  • sites.google.com
Forwarding your domain to your host

If you choose a free hosting solution but want to use your own custom domain name, you will need to do a process called "forwarding". You can call the support line where you bought the domain name, and they can guide you through this process.

Advertising your website

Inbound marketing to ease users through your funnel

While your users are evaluating whether to make the choice you want them to make on your site, they should be getting value. You will need to keep their attention long enough for them to arrive at as many calls to action as possible. As your user travels down the page, you should be supplying them with useful information interspersed with calls to action. and a secondary value proposition and call to action. That way, at any point of the funnel, a user is not far from the choice you want them to make. The art of it comes into it trying to find the thin line between not getting in the way of people who know what they want, and pushing your sale on people who need more convincing.

Relying on more than just word of mouth

It may be tempting to rely on spreading your website by talking to people about it. While it is important to notify your community about updates to your services, you can't rely solely on talking about your website to promote it. In fact, one of the main reasons why people make websites is to remove the need to describe your business to one person at a time. If you are able to personally address your users, I would recommend just doing that. If you’re doing something that people are talking about a lot already, then that can be harnessed with a website. If that’s not already happening for you, don’t count on it starting once you’ve made a website.

Search Engine Optimizaiton

SEO is important when a search engine is deciding which websites to display from a search, but it can’t automatically put you at the top of that list. Most of the methods that people use aren’t an exact science, because the search engine company is constantly making changes to find better sites and finding ways to prevent you from cheating it. Search engines are constantly scraping through new websites to find relevant content to provide their users. Some standard practice methods to becoming more available to search engines:

  • Optimize engagement on your site
  • Provide content designed for accessibility concerns
  • Have other sites link to yours (backlinks)
  • Include meta information for all your pages and content
Augmenting your SEO with paid advertising

Good placement on search engines is very dependent on if your site is already bonafide (for example, when users don’t try other search results after yours - suggesting they are satisfied with your site). So it can be very important for new sites to artificially place themselves higher in the results before they can become bonafide. For this reason, many new businesses choose to start paid advertisements.

For example, google ads will let you bid for the top search result. Bids happen every time a user searches for keywords that you designate. You can change your settings to pay more or less, based on how relevant the specific search was to you. You can also set limits on how much you are willing to spend in a day. Facebook has an interesting advertising structure as well, allowing you to pay based on how many times your page is shown or how many times someone actually clicks on your ad.

Imagining your ideal customer

Before purchasing an ad, there are some important marketing exercises to go through. First, try to imagine very specific characteristics of your ideal customer. The old fashioned method is to actually write out the characteristics of a handful of realistic seeming people. If you like, you can create many profiles, and name them and everything. In order to prepare for targeting your ads, try to at least come up with:

  • What interaction they take with your site (purchase, email list, contact, etc.)
  • Physical location (or more general: city, state, country, etc.)
  • Age (or age range)
  • Interests/hobbies (imagine if you will)
Preparing to purchase your ad

Setting up the actual ad (on google ads, for example) is relatively cut and dry. The real finesse comes in by creating the perfect messaging for the perfect person. Even if you’ve already imagined the perfect customer, that raises some important questions:

  • How many people exist who match my perfect customer?
  • What’s the exact wording that my perfect customer would use?
  • What distinguishes my services (for them) from their other options?
  • As my ad is being shown to people, how can I monitor what works well?

My favorite tool for investigating target markets is trends.google.com . You can observe the volume of searches based on location, so you can figure out the best wording for your ad. For example, maybe “cupcake” gets less searches than “muffin”, so your bakery company might want to consider muffin.

The mechanics behind ad auctions

When you create a campaign for your ad, you designate things like when to show the ad and how much you allot to spend on the ad per day. This allows your ad provider to choose an ad whenever a relevant search happens. For example, if multiple people are all buying ads for the keywords “muffin”, “bakery”, and “gingerbread”, then google needs to be able to choose which ad shows when somebody searches for muffin!

You can change your campaign settings to budget more or less per ad and per day. You also have the option to pay per click, rather than per visualization.

Incorporating feedback into further campaigns

As your ad campaign progresses, you will be able monitor progress. It is paramount to constantly learn from campaigns, and adjust as necessary. You’ll never hit a bullseye on a target you can’t see. It is also important to note you can view ad traffic as separate from normal traffic, so you can see exactly what you’re getting from ad-generated visits to your site.
